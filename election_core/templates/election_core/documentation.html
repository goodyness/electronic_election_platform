FlashVote Ecosystem

The definitive guide to secure, institutional digital democracy.

Getting Started

FlashVote is designed for scale and integrity. To begin, institutions must be registered on the grid. Once an institution (University, College, or Association) is created, authorized personnel can sign up as Election Organizers.

Key Requirement

All organizers must wait for Grand Admin Authorization before they can create active election sessions.

Organizer Operations

1. Mission Parameters

Define election titles, descriptions, and strict start/end windows. Use the "War Room" to monitor live turnouts once the election goes live.

2. Voter Grid

Upload authenticated voter lists via bulk email CSV or registration number sets. FlashVote severely restricts access to only those on the pre-approved ledger.

3. Candidate Pool

Create positions and attach candidates with high-resolution imagery and manifests. Note: Editing is locked once voting commences.

4. Settlement

Select from Free, Standard, or Premium tiers. Premium tiers unlock real-time OTP delivery via high-priority email relays.

The Voter Experience

OTP
Auth Protocol

Accreditation Flow

Voters enter their email/ID. If matched on the ledger, a unique 6-digit One Time Password is generated and sent to their verified email. This ensures every ballot is verified by the voter's primary communication channel. OTP codes can only be sent a maximum of 2 times per session to prevent abuse.

Ballot Privacy

Once the OTP is verified, the voter enters the virtual polling booth. Our "Severance" protocol ensures that while we track that you HAVE voted, we never record HOW you voted in a way that links it back to your identity.

Security & Audit

Integrity

Action Logging

Every administrative action (Pause, Resume, Extend) is logged with a mandatory reason, visible in the final audit report.

Verification

Ballot Ledger

Voters receive a cryptographic "I Voted" asset. They can use the "Ballot Verification" module to prove their vote was counted without revealing their choice.

Output

Immutable PDF

Official results are only available once an election is closed, exported as a signed, tamper-evident PDF document.

Public Audit & Integrity

FlashVote implements a SHA-256 Audit Hash Publication system. When an election is closed and sealed, a deterministic hash is generated from the complete, ordered result set. This hash is published publicly and can be verified by anyone — no login required.

How It Works

Audit Hash Generation

  1. Election is closed by the organizer
  2. All votes are serialized in deterministic order
  3. A SHA-256 hash is computed from the serialized data
  4. The hash is stored and published on the results page
Verification

Public Audit Portal

Anyone can visit the Public Audit page and enter an Election ID or Receipt ID to verify that the results haven't been tampered with.

Integrity Guarantee

If even a single vote is added, removed, or modified after sealing, the hash will completely change — making tampering immediately detectable.

Troubleshooting

I didn't receive my OTP code

Check your spam/junk folder first. OTP codes expire after 5 minutes. You can request a maximum of 2 OTPs per session. If you still haven't received it, contact your election organizer.

The election says voting is not enabled

The organizer controls when voting opens and closes. Ensure the election's start time has passed and that the organizer has enabled voting from their management dashboard.

I want to verify my vote was counted

After voting, you'll receive a "I Voted" badge and a receipt ID. Visit the Public Audit page and enter your receipt ID to confirm your participation was recorded.
